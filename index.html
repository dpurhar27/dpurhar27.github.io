Why we use the blockchain

Security

Our platform uses a non-custodial approach. Users hold their funds in their own wallets controlled by their private keys and never on our platform. This protects user funds from ever being stolen by the method centralized exchanges have severly suffered which has resulted in a loss of about $80 million in user funds since 2017.


Accessability

Traditional financial markets require intermediaries adding cost and complexity. Centralized authority censor and take people's rights away. Our platform uses the Ethereum blockchain as a base layer. This means it is run by computers globally located. Collateral is based in cryptocurrency. These both make our market accessible to anyone anywhere.

User Privacy

Consumer awareness of the depth of data mining and selling has been raised from privacy breaches like the recent Facebook scandal. A global cultural change has began as users demand ownership and privacy of their data. We don't sell any personal information from our users. We use collected market data to help us analyze trends, gain insights and improve our markets with A.I.

Transparency

Every position and trade is settled and stored on the blockchain which is public and immutable. This allows users to verify that our platform is honest. The integrity of different componenets like the liquidation process on a centralized platform is questioned by its users. We take away this worry by using the blockchain to verify states before any action and emit them after for the user to check.

Trustless

Our platform logic is written in smart contracts which are executed on the blockchain. Smart contracts allows for trustless execution of logic like the liquidation process or settlement of trades. This creates a trustless market where users don't worry about how the market is behaving and enables the transparency that traditional markets can't have. This also lowers the cost of the market and allows for cheaper fees.

Decentralized

Key aspects such as trade settlement and collateral storage are done on-chain. The control of user assets are not reliant on central entities. This avoids manipulation or dishonest behaviour as users don't rely on a central entity to resolve their trades. The redundancy of the blockchain also ensures our market is always online and won't freeze assets in highly-volatile times.

Use Cases of our Market

These are some of the use cases of our derivatives market. All in which our market need to be used over a buy/sell exchange. We allow users controlled exposure to risk so they can protect themselves from it. Our market allows users to profit whether their assets are increasing or losing value.
Shorting/Longing Functionality
    When users believe an asset will go down or up in value they can use our market to profit whether they hold the asset or not. While going long is similar to buying, selling is not equivalent to shorting. With shorting you make profit from the asset going down. Compared to selling where you avoid losing money, aside from the selling fees and buying fees if you purchase the asset again.
Hedging Functionality
    Hedging is a technique to mitigate loss of capital. When users hold assets in their portfolio they believe will go down in value short-term they can short on our market rather then sell on an exchange and buy back later. This protects their portfolio from losing value and saves the user fee charges. The user can also apply this for long-term situations and the opposite case where they sell their asset and go long on our market.
Leverage Functionality
    Leverage works by borrowing margin. In our inital market design, the user borrows margin from us. Whether longing/shorting or hedging, users can apply leverage ranging from 2x to 100x on their positions. This means users can amplify their profits up to 100x. Ex. If somebody successfully closes a position for a nominal $5 profit but used 100x leverage, they would actually receive a profit of $500. Few exchanges offer leverage options, and those that do offer the highest at 25x.
